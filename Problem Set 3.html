1) Let X, Y, and Z be independent RVs with variances σX , σY , σZ . Let:¶

\begin{equation} U=ZX \\ V=ZY \end{equation}

Answer the following questions:¶

(a) Find Cov(U, V) and U,V

(b)If U=Z+X and V=Z+Y, do the values of Cov(U,V)and U,V computed inpart a) change? Explain.

(c) How does U,V change if ^2_Z is much larger than ^2_X or ^2_Y?

(d) How does U,V change if ^2_Z is much smaller than ^2_X or ^2_Y?

(e) How do the answers for parts c) and d) relate to variable standardization?

a) \begin{equation} Cov(U,V) = E[UV] - E[U]E[V] = E[(Z-X)(Z-Y)] - E[Z-X]E[Z-Y] \\ = E[Z^2 - ZY - ZX + XY] - (E[Z]-E[X])(E[Z]-E[Y]) \\ = E[Z^2] - E[ZY] - E[ZX] + E[XY] - E[Z]^2 - E[Z]E[Y] + E[Z]E[X] - E[X]E[Y] \\ = Var(Z) - Cov(XZ) - Cov(YZ) - Cov(XY) = Var(Z) = \sigma^2_Z \end{equation}

\begin{equation} Var(U) = Z-X = Var(Z) - Var(X) = \sigma^2_{Z} - \sigma^2_{X} \\ Var(V) = \sigma^2_Z - \sigma^2_Y \\ \therefore \rho_{U,V} = \frac{\sigma^2_z}{\sqrt{(\sigma^2_Z - \sigma^2_X)(\sigma^2_Z - \sigma^2_y)}} \end{equation}

b) No, they are still independent and shifting has no impact to their covariance and correlation.

c) As the variance of Z gets bigger than variance of U and V, the correlation of U and V will approach 1.

d) As the variance of Z gets smaller than variance of U and V, the correlation of U and V will approach 0 (uncorrelated).

e) Variance is a standard unit because it has no units. In addition, cov is also a standard unit, thus we can use the two forms of standard units to find our correlation which is also a standard unit.
