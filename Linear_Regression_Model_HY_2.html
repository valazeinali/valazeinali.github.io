Manger A had a correlation of .66 to HY¶

Manager B had a correlation of .49 to HY¶

Manager C had a correlation of .52 to HY¶

We notice that Manager A had the highest correlation to the SPY500 and also had the highest exposure to HY Credit.¶

However, we notice that Manager B and C had moderate exposure to HY Credit, but had a low correlation to SPY500.¶

Depending on how you look at it, AQR's hypothesis can go both ways. We also need to keep in mind that AQR said "most managers" and not all.¶

Our results simply show that moderate correlation to HY resulted in lower correlation to SPY and higher correlation to HY resulted in higher correlation to SPY.¶
